Welcome to my webpage. I am a researcher studying the cognitive science of language. Currently, I'm an MA student in the Linguistics programme at the Department of English, National Taiwan Normal University. There, I work in the Neurolinguistics Lab directed by Prof. Shiaohui Chan.

Here's one of my favourite science quotes which I think is really important. It was written by the linguist Louis Hjelmslev in his famous Prolegomena to a Theory of Language but it expresses a general message which applies to any scientific theory, not just theories in linguistics. Personally, I think the idea that the things we isolate as "objects" are really comprised of relationships between relationships was best developed by Alfred North Whitehead in his Process Philosophy school of thought, but Hjelmslev did a better job articulating the idea in one sentence.

"The postulation of objects as something different from the terms of relationships is a superfluous axiom and consequently a metaphysical hypothesis from which linguistic science will have to be freed."
— Louis Hjelmslev
